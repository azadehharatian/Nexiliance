CRA Requires a Software Bill of Materials (SBOM)
Under CRA Article 13(3), manufacturers must identify and document all software components in their products. An SBOM is the equivalent of a list of ingredients for your software — it must be maintained but does not need to be published.

SBOM Integration Steps

1
Choose your SBOM tool
Select Syft, Trivy, or a language-specific tool (e.g. cyclonedx-npm, cyclonedx-maven). Ensure it outputs SPDX or CycloneDX format.
2
Generate your first SBOM
Run syft path/to/image -o cyclonedx-json or equivalent. Verify all components are captured.
3
Integrate into CI/CD
Add SBOM generation to every build pipeline. Store the SBOM as a build artifact. Update on every release.
4
Set up continuous CVE monitoring
Use Trivy, Grype, or Dependabot to scan your SBOM components against the NVD/OSV databases for newly disclosed CVEs.
5
Store securely — no publication required
CRA Article 13(3) does not require publishing your SBOM, but it must be available to market surveillance authorities upon request.

CRA Reporting Obligation — Effective September 2026
Under CRA Articles 13(6) and 14, manufacturers must report actively exploited vulnerabilities to ENISA within 24 hours of discovery. Severe security incidents must also be reported. A new ENISA reporting platform will be established for this purpose.
